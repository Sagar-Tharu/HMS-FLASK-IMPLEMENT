Hospital Privacy and Policy

Key Findings

1. Prevalence of Tracking Technologies

Nearly all hospitalsâ€™ websites (96%) collect and transmit user information to third parties, yet most do not have privacy policies that disclose exactly who will receive this data.

2. Disclosure of Data Collection and Use

While 97.2% of websites disclosed the types of user information being collected, 98.6% disclosed how the information was being used, and 93% disclosed the types of third parties receiving the data.

3. Regulatory Risks

Hospitals may face regulatory risks if their published website privacy policies fall short of Federal Trade Commission (FTC) standards.

4. Importance of Clear and Concise Policies

Privacy policies are often time-consuming to read and difficult to understand, providing an imperfect solution for protecting user privacy.

Recommendations

1. Prioritize Transparency

Hospitals should clearly disclose their use of third-party tracking technologies and the recipients of user data.

2. Improve Policy Clarity

Privacy policies should be concise, easily understandable, and regularly reviewed and updated to ensure compliance with regulatory standards.

3. Implement Opt-In Mechanisms

Hospitals should consider implementing alternative solutions, such as opt-in consent mechanisms, to better protect user privacy.
